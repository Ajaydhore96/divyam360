Speciality & Business Insurance

Speciality and business insurance Pune covers complex, high-value and industry-specific risks that standard policies do not address. At Divyam360, your trusted commercial insurance advisor Pune, we help businesses protect their operations, assets and liabilities with tailored solutions.

From marine cargo insurance Pune to fire and allied insurance Pune, liability insurance Maharashtra and cyber insurance for business India, our advisors work with leading insurers to structure the right cover for your business � ensuring you are protected against the risks that matter most.

Marine Insurance

Marine insurance covers goods in transit by sea, air, road or rail against loss or damage. It is essential for importers, exporters and logistics companies to protect their cargo and hull assets.

  • Coverage for goods in transit by all modes of transport
  • Institute Cargo Clauses A, B and C options
  • Open cover policy for regular shippers
  • War and strike risks cover available
  • Prompt survey and claim settlement support

Fire Insurance

Fire insurance protects your business premises, stock, plant and machinery from fire, explosion, lightning and allied perils. It is a fundamental cover for any commercial establishment or industrial unit.

  • Coverage for fire, lightning, explosion and implosion
  • Flood, storm, cyclone and earthquake add-ons available
  • Covers building, plant, machinery, stock and furniture
  • Business interruption cover for loss of profit
  • Reinstatement value basis for full replacement cost

Liability Insurance

Liability insurance protects your business from legal claims arising from bodily injury, property damage or professional errors caused to third parties. It is essential for businesses of all sizes operating in India.

  • Public liability cover for third-party bodily injury and property damage
  • Product liability for manufacturers and distributors
  • Professional indemnity for consultants, doctors and IT firms
  • Directors and Officers (D&O) liability cover
  • Legal defence costs covered in addition to damages

Commercial Insurance

Commercial insurance is a comprehensive package policy designed for businesses. It combines multiple covers - property, liability, business interruption and employee benefits � under a single policy for convenience and cost efficiency.

  • Package policy combining multiple business covers
  • Property damage, theft and business interruption included
  • Employee fidelity and money insurance options
  • Customisable based on industry and business size
  • Suitable for retail, manufacturing, hospitality and service businesses

Cyber Insurance

Cyber insurance protects businesses from financial losses arising from data breaches, ransomware attacks, cyber fraud and system failures. As digital threats grow, cyber cover has become essential for businesses of all sizes.

  • Coverage for data breach response and notification costs
  • Ransomware and cyber extortion cover
  • Business interruption due to cyber attack
  • Third-party liability for data privacy breaches
  • Cyber fraud and social engineering cover

  • Do small businesses need liability insurance?

    Yes. Even small businesses face risks from third-party claims. Public liability and professional indemnity insurance protect you from legal costs and compensation claims that could otherwise be financially devastating.

  • Is cyber insurance necessary for my business?

    If your business handles customer data, processes online payments or relies on digital systems, cyber insurance is strongly recommended. Cyber attacks are increasing rapidly and the financial impact of a breach can be severe.

  • What is the difference between marine cargo and marine hull insurance?

    Marine cargo insurance covers goods being transported, while marine hull insurance covers the vessel itself. Most importers and exporters need cargo insurance; shipping companies need hull insurance.

  • What does fire insurance cover for a business?

    Fire insurance for businesses covers the building structure, plant and machinery, stock, furniture and fixtures against fire, lightning, explosion, riots and allied perils. A comprehensive policy can also include flood, earthquake and business interruption cover to protect against loss of income during the restoration period.

  • What is professional indemnity insurance?

    Professional indemnity (PI) insurance protects professionals � doctors, architects, consultants, IT companies and chartered accountants � against claims of negligence, errors or omissions in their professional services. If a client suffers a financial loss due to your advice or work, PI insurance covers legal defence costs and compensation.

  • What does a commercial general liability policy cover?

    A commercial general liability (CGL) policy covers third-party bodily injury and property damage claims arising from your business operations, products or premises. It pays for legal defence costs, settlements and court awards. It is essential for manufacturers, retailers, contractors and service businesses that interact with the public.

  • What does cyber insurance cover?

    Cyber insurance covers financial losses from data breaches, ransomware attacks, hacking and cyber extortion. It typically includes costs for IT forensics, data recovery, legal liability to affected customers, regulatory fines, business interruption losses and crisis communication. As cyber threats grow, this cover is increasingly essential for any business with digital operations.

  • Who needs a Contractors All Risk (CAR) policy?

    CAR insurance is essential for contractors, builders, project owners and developers involved in civil, mechanical or electrical construction projects. It covers damage to the works under construction, construction equipment and third-party liability during the project period. Most project contracts and bank loans for construction require a CAR policy.

  • Can a startup or small business afford speciality insurance?

    Yes. Many speciality insurance products are available at affordable premiums for small businesses and startups. The cost of a claim � legal fees, compensation, data breach response � far exceeds the annual premium. Divyam360 helps startups and SMEs identify the most critical risks and choose cost-effective cover that fits their budget.

  • How is the sum insured determined for a fire insurance policy?

    The sum insured for fire insurance should reflect the reinstatement value � the cost to rebuild or replace the insured property at current market rates, not the market value. Under-insuring leads to proportional claim deductions (average clause). Divyam360 helps businesses calculate the correct sum insured to avoid under-insurance penalties at claim time.
