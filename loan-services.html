  • What documents are required for a loan application?

    Basic documents include: Aadhaar card, PAN card, salary slips (last 3 months), bank statements (last 6 months), Form 16, and property documents (for secured loans). Self-employed individuals need GST returns, ITR, and business proof.

  • What credit score is needed for loan approval?

    A CIBIL score of 750 or above is considered excellent for loan approval. Scores between 650-749 may get approval with higher interest rates. Below 650, approval chances are lower, but we can guide you on improving your credit score.

  • How long does loan approval take?

    Personal loans can be approved within 24-48 hours. Home and car loans typically take 3-7 working days. Business loans may take 1-2 weeks depending on documentation and property valuation.

  • Can I foreclose my loan before tenure ends?

    Yes, most home loans allow foreclosure after 6-12 months without charges. Personal loans may have prepayment charges of 2-4% in the first year. Car and business loans vary by lender. We recommend loans with minimal or zero foreclosure penalties.

  • What is EMI and how is it calculated?

    EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed amount you pay monthly to repay your loan. It includes both principal and interest. Use our Loan EMI Calculator to calculate your monthly payment based on loan amount, interest rate, and tenure.

  • Can I apply for multiple loans simultaneously?

    Yes, you can have multiple loans (e.g., home loan + car loan) simultaneously, provided your total EMI-to-income ratio doesn't exceed 50-60%. Lenders check your repayment capacity before approval. Divyam360 helps you structure loans optimally to maintain healthy finances.

  • What is a good EMI-to-income ratio?

    Financial experts recommend keeping your total EMI payments below 40-50% of your monthly income. This ensures you have sufficient funds for other expenses and savings. We provide personalized financial planning to help you maintain optimal debt levels.

  • What tax benefits does life insurance offer?

    Premiums paid for life insurance are eligible for deduction under Section 80C up to ?1.5 lakh per year. The death benefit received by the nominee is tax-free under Section 10(10D). Maturity proceeds are also tax-exempt subject to conditions. Divyam360 helps you maximise these tax benefits.

  • How do I choose the right policy term?

    The policy term should ideally cover your working years � typically until age 60�65. If you have dependents, a home loan or business liabilities, extend the term to cover those obligations fully. A longer term locks in lower premiums at a younger age.

  • What is a retirement or pension plan?

    A retirement or pension plan is a life insurance product that helps you build a corpus during your working years and provides a regular income (annuity) after retirement. It ensures financial independence in old age without depending on family or savings alone. Divyam360 helps you choose between deferred annuity, immediate annuity and NPS-linked plans.
